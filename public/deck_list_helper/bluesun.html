Intro:  Blue Sun is part of Weyland Consortium, notorious for blowing up problems rather than trying to fix them. They produce so much power and credits that they are able to make use of the toughest ICE from all the mega corporations to stop the runner from infiltrating their data. They're very "old money"; throw credits at it until they get exactly what they want.

Strengths:  Incredible credit generation, strongest ICE in the game

Weaknesses:  You need money to make money, all ICE are expensive

Playstyle:  Blue Sun is very classic netrunner; make a lot of credits, then rez big and strong ICE to keep runners out. Through their ID ability, you can guarantee Corporate Wars from firing, having enough credits for Hedge Funds and Restructures, and bouncing back Elizabeth Mills and utilizing her people skills to remove bad publicity.
Bryan Stinson is your best friend; use him often when the runner is low on credits so that you always have enough to rez your big and expensive ICE.
Will-o'-the-Wisp will help keep runners out of your servers when you fire it at a key icebreaker. There are a lot of very expensive and taxing barriers in this deck, so targeting the runner's fracter is usually a good idea.
Be aggressive in your game plan with Blue Sun. Find an expensive ICE to advance agendas behind of and score some early points. Mid game will be where you will have to set up some of your strongest ICE, then hopefully you'll be able to close it out by late game

Mulligan:  Spiderweb is one of the cheapest ICE that can stop the runner early game. A hand full of ICE and credit-generating cards is always a safe keep.

Tips:  Bouncing back Elizabeth Mills to remove your bad publicity will be a good strategy to use. The deck can generate quite a few, so taking a couple turns removing them can really help your mid to late game.
Sandburg can absolutely destroy the runner's chances of getting through your servers if you have enough credits thanks to Bryan Stinson.
Don't be afraid of placing Curtain Walls early in the game if it can score you a lot of points. You can bounce it back later to always make use of its positional power.
If you have enough credits and the runner has no sentry killer, Shinobi can straight up flatline the runner by itself; always check how much link they have before committing to your traces!
