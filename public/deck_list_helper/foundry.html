Intro:  The Foundry is a division of Haas-Bioroid that mines the materials needed to create bioroids. Though they don't use bioroids themselves, they make use of their NEXT line (Bronze - Silver - Gold) of ICE, as well as the Grail line (Galahad - Lancelot - Excalibur - Merlin) of ICE to protect their assets. While there are many clearance levels (Green - Blue - Ultraviolet - Black) when stationed at The Foundry, you luckily, possess all of them.

Strengths:  Strong potential to flatline the runner, click efficient clearance cards to get you cards and credits

Weaknesses:  NEXT line requires multiple to become a threat, potential to overdraw when using clearance cards

Playstyle:  The Foundry has a lot of cool synergy with its ICE package. The entire NEXT line benefits each other the more rezzed NEXT ICE there are, while the Grail line benefits each other when some are kept in HQ. Not only does the ID ability benefit your defenses, it also helps to filter through your deck when using clearance cards to draw.
The glacier style (having a large remote server with lots of ICE) is very prevalent here, but it's not the only way in which to win. Through the use of Corporate Troubleshooter and The Twins, you can set up a flatline situation when a runner runs into a NEXT Gold or any of the Grail ICE with Merlin(s) kept in HQ.
If the runner runs into an advanced Cerebral Overwriter, it makes the flatline potential even greater and causes hesitation and caution during their future runs. Make use of this hesitation by further scoring agendas while they take clicks to catch up.

Mulligan:  Try to look for NEXT Bronze or Silver to shore up early vulnerabilites, Cybernetics Court to prevent overdraw from clearance cards.
Alternatively, you can try to set up a turn 1-2 flatline when a Merlin (ideally 2) show up in your starting hand.

Tips:  Jeeves works well with your clearance cards. When spending a click or clicks to play operations, if a total of 3 clicks were spent, Jeeves will trigger to gain you an extra click. That means even if you just play a single Ultraviolet Clearance, Jeeves will trigger.
Always be on the look out for a potential flatline, but don't make that your first priority. Do so when the opportunity presents itself while still advancing your agendas. This puts more pressure on the runner which often leads to more mistakes, ultimately leading to an easier flatline scenario.
