Intro:  Geist is a one and done kind of runner. Each program, hardware, icebreaker, and resource will have its use, and once he's done with it, he'll never use it again. Luckily he has a lot of things at his disposal.

Strengths:  Incredible ways to filter and burn through his deck

Weaknesses:  Cloud icebreakers are highly dependent on each other

Playstyle:  Geist is an avalanche of a runner. It takes quite a while to set up his rig, but once things start rolling, they really start rolling. A lot of his gameplay will be drawing and installing, with a certain point in your gameplay to be trashing the installed cards to draw with his ability, then installing, condensing a bit of your clicks economy.
Take a couple turns setting things up, such as getting some help from Street Peddlers and Technical Writers to start filtering through your deck and gaining some economy at the same time. Once you have a couple of Cloud breakers (Spike - Crowbar - Shiv) or Grappling Hooks alongside an e3 Feedback Implants, you'll be able to smoothly break almost anything in your way.
Since your rig is mostly disposable, you'll need to run smart, that is to say you must figure out the most opportune time to strike at a remote threatening an agenda. With the Cloud breakers, you will mostly be limited on the number of runs you can make a turn. However, do not be shy just because they are limited. With the help of Levy AR Lab Access you'll be able to start burning through your deck all over again.

Mulligan:  Try to look for e3 Feedback Implants with some economy such as Tech Traders or Technical Writers.

Tips:  Installing Spy Cameras, albeit slow, are a great way of scrying for certain cards (once you have a couple that is).
One of the best uses for e3 Feedback Implants is against Bioroid ICE. When you spend click(s) to break their subroutines, you can then trigger e3 to break the rest without spending additional clicks!
