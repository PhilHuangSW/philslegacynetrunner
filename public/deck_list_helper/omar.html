Intro:  Omar is an old school runner with some new tricks. There is nothing super flashy about his style, everything is just solid from his experience.

Strengths:  Deep digging when Surging your Mediums and Nerve Agents. Icebreaker destruction is not a factor when they can be brought back from the heap

Weaknesses:  No reliable economy package, can be slow to build back up after a virus purging

Playstyle:  Omar can definitely surprise the corp with how explosive he can get. One clean Frantic Coding session and he's off to the races. An ideal hit would be Paperclip, Black Orchestra, MKUltra, Medium, and Nerve Agent chucked into the bin with a Deja Vu to bring back Medium and Nerve Agent; the icebreakers can bring themselves back into play with their ability.
Once everything is setup, just run either HQ or R&D and Surge the respective viruses to get massive accesses.
One of the best things about running with Omar is that you can learn how to be a smarter runner by being reckless. After you have Street Magic out, or a couple of your icebreakers are installed/in the bin, you can just freely run into ICE and seeing that, while scary initially, ICE aren't really that harmful.
The only real problem with Omar is that he doesn't have a reliable credit engine. You can do yourself a favor though by Stimhacking into a server with ICE that allows you to bring back your icebreakers from the heap.

Mulligan:  Look for a Frantic Coding and Deja Vu in your opening hand, it can get you set up pretty much from turn 1.

Tips:  Don't be afraid to use both of your Frantic Codings, with the help of Trope and Deja Vu, you should be able to just bring back any piece(s) you need.
