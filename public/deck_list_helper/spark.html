Intro:  Spark Agency is part of NBN, the mega corporation that controls the world's media. Spark acquires its talents and rapidly spreads ads and news so as to get you to purchase immediately. They want to make sure you use your credits on their products, and not on programs that hack into their database.

Strengths:  Lots of ways of draining the runner of their credits

Weaknesses:  Not the strongest ICE, ID can only trigger once per turn

Playstyle:  Spark is all about economy denial; a broke runner (usually) means safe servers. A lot of your assets both gain you credits as well as drain their credits. By rezzing an Adonis Campaign (or any other advertisement) at the end of the runner's turn, you simultaneously trigger your ID as well as enter your turn with the drip economy running. This credit swing (although initially slow), can force the runner into difficult or unexpected decisions.
Agendas such as NAPD Contract, 15 Minutes, and Explode-a-palooza can be beneficial to you when the runner scores them. Placing them out in new naked remotes alongside some ad campaigns can throw off the runner off their gameplan.
SanSan City Grid is one of your most powerful tools to win. Placing one alongside another facedown card really puts the runner in a pickle; do they run through that server (hopefully being protected by a Tollbooth) to catch the agenda, or have they wasted credits by running straight into another ad campaign? Not only that, it's extremely expensive to trash and will usually give you another opportunity to score when the runner does decide to trash it.

Mulligan:  Mull for SanSan City Grids alongside ICE and ad campaigns.

Tips:  Once Rebranding Team has been scored, all assets have the advertisement tag, so Ad Blitz and your ID can target any asset.
Make use of the "first time each turn" of your ID, that is to say you can rez an advertisement on your turn, force the runner to lose a credit, then rez another advertisement on the runner's turn to force them to lose another credit.
Early Premiere will help you score out a 4/2 if you placed an agenda to look like an ad campaign. Read what the runner will do, and test your limits!
