Intro:  Haas-Bioroid is the corporation that created the bioroids; androids built with cybernetic technology with artificially-intelligent minds designed around sophisticated imaging of human brains. They've made an enormous amount of money by exploiting cheap android labor and by constantly pushing bioroid campaigns to the public.

Strengths:  Efficient strength/cost ratio on ICE through their ability, strong drip economy by pushing campaigns

Weaknesses:  All ICE are bioroids which can be broken with remaining clicks, economy requires time to receive

Playstyle:  Haas-Bioroid is classic glacier; that is you build large servers with lots of ICE, then score behind the wall of ICE. With the numerous amounts of different campaigns, it is an absolute chore for runners to run each campaign and pay the high trash cost to get rid of them.
Spend the early game shoring up your servers with ICE while hopefully making use of your ID. Putting out all sorts of campaigns presents a catch-22 for the runner; will they spend clicks and credits to trash them or allow you to slowly gain a credit advantage as time goes on. Once you have established a healthy credit pool, your general path to victory will be to keep scoring behind a 2+ ICED up server.

Mulligan:  Your ideal hand would contain a couple of campaigns (Marilyn - Adonis) with some fairly cheap bioroids (Eli 1.0 - Fairchild 1.0) to snowball into stronger bioroids (Heimdall 1.0 - Fairchild - Ichi 2.0) through your ID.

Tips:  Use Biotic Labor to score 3 advancement agendas (Sensor Net Activation - Project Vitruvius - Director Haas' Pet Project) from hand, or an Advanced Concept Hopper if installed a previous turn. The high cost will easily be recouped if you have plenty of campaigns running.
If a glacier server is already set up and the runner can threaten that remote, try baiting them to run by installing a campaign last click. If they run it, they'll waste their credits on the expensive to break bioroids while also not getting anything when they run headfirst into a campaign.
