Intro:  Adam is just searching for his way. He was programmed to be a Netrunner along with his Three Directives (Safety First - Always Be Running - Neutralize All Threats), but, why? With the help and guidance of some powerful Deva AI, his goal is to find out, all the while being compulsed to hack.

Strengths:  Powerful AI icebreakers, a plethora of recurring credits for icebreakers and events

Weaknesses:  Must follow his initial Three Directives which can force you into awkward situations

Playstyle:  Adam is great to build your running skills as it forces you to do and play out of your comfort zone, developing good practices as a runner in general.
First Directive: Safety First seems like a pretty big set back, but being able to draw a card every turn for free essentially is insane. Once you have Brain Chip installed and a couple agendas stolen, the drawback to Safety First is straight nullified, while still having the awesome draw engine all game.
Second Directive: Always Be Running is definitely terrifying for a beginner runner, but you'll find through experience, running into ICE with no breakers isn't so bad. Most of the consequences you handle in the early game can be dealt with crediting back up or drawing back up. Being able to break any ICE subroutine for just two clicks is awesome and will force the corp to have to install more ICE to defend against your constant onslaught.
Third Directive: Neutralize All Threats is almost always good. You'll want to trash all of the corps' cards anyway, so being able to access an additional card in HQ is all upside for you. It also only works for the first card with a trash cost, so anything you access afterward, you don't need to trash it if it's too damaging.
Now with the Directives accounted for, you'll want to have all of your recurring credits set up (Multithreader - Cyberfeeder - Prepaid VoicePAD) then just run using all of your AI icebreakers. Dr. Lovegood is there to patch you up in case you really can't afford to follow one of your Directives.

Mulligan:  Hard mull for Brain Chip.

Tips:  Using Brahman after all of your Overmind power counters are spent is the best way to utilize Overmind over and over again.
