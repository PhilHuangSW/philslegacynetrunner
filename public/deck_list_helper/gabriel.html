Intro:  Gabe is the consummate professional, he knows just about everyone, and everyone knows him. He is the John Wick of Netrunner, with his favorite icebreaker being the very fitting, all-powerful Baba Yaga.

Strengths:  For a Criminal, he has an incredible breaker suite if installed onto Baba Yaga. With all his connections, he has a lot of ways to gain credits, and sometimes even clicks

Weaknesses:  For being so reputable, Gabe takes a while to set up. While Baba Yaga is incredible, it requires all of its supporting cast installed on to it in order for it to be the killing machine it's known for

Playstyle:  Playing this deck is kind of like going through the John Wick movies. Meeting a whole bunch of connections to get you through rough patches while digging up all your icebreakers to then shoot up all the corp's ICE.
Both Baba Yaga and Off-Campus Apartment play very similarly. Both of them will require you to install them first before your other icebreakers/resources. The more icebreakers on Baba Yaga, the deadlier and more efficient it becomes. Off-Campus Apartment is there to help get things rolling faster.
It is important to know that all of your central-only icebreakers (Alias - Breach - Passport) placed onto Baba Yaga will allow Baba Yaga to use their abilities without the central-only restriction! This makes for very efficient breaking at the cost of finding all the pieces in the right order.

Mulligan:  Hard mull for Baba Yaga and Off-Campus Apartment. If you start with one and have either Hostage and/or Special Order, that is a good keep as well.

Tips:  Hostage targets include Beth Kilrain-Chang, Kati Jones, The Supplier, and Rachel Beckman (once one of your Decoys has been set up).
